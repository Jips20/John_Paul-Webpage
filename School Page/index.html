About Our School

The University of Manila upholds its core ideals: Patria (love of country), Scientia (knowledge), and Virtus (virtue), which are reflected in its seal.

The olive wreath represents honor, the triangle symbolizes the unity of the three ideals, and the open book stands for continuous learning. The burning torch signifies passion and determination, while the colors green and white represent innovation and purity.

The university’s mascot, the Hawk, symbolizes strength and determination in achieving goals.

History

On October 5, 1913, five young students met in Intramuros, Manila, driven by a dream to create a school for Filipino youth. Led by Dr. Apolinario G. de los Santos, they founded Instituto de Manila, which opened in 1914 offering elementary and high school education.

As the school grew, it transferred to Sampaloc and expanded its facilities. In 1921, it was reorganized and became the University of Manila. The institution continued to develop, adding various colleges such as Law, Liberal Arts, Education, Business Administration, and Engineering, along with graduate programs.

The College of Law gained recognition for producing topnotchers in the Bar Examinations, establishing the university’s reputation for excellence. Despite challenges, including world wars, the university continued to grow and adapt to societal changes.

Under successive leaders, especially the de los Santos family, the university expanded its programs, introduced modern courses, and strengthened its academic standards. New degrees in fields like Computer Science, Public Administration, and Business Administration were offered to meet modern demands.

In recent years, the university has improved its facilities, gained accreditations, built international partnerships, and provided scholarships to support students. It continues to uphold its ideals—Patria, Scientia, et Virtus—aiming to deliver quality education and contribute to society.

Today, the University of Manila stands as a respected institution committed to academic excellence, innovation, and service to the Filipino community.

Philosophy of Education

Mission

ACE — to accomplish something with complete success

  • A — anchor in the hearts of the youth the love of country, science and virtue
  • C — create an atmosphere of academic excellence and competence
  • E — empower the youth with modern scientific technological skills in braving the challenges of the global society

Vision

The University of Manila envisions a dynamic community that is deeply steeped in the trilogy of UM ideals: love of country, love of science and love of virtue; fully equipped with human, conceptual and technological skills; firmly determined to make life for others, better, brighter, lighter and more livable, by making quality education accessible to all.

Motto

To acquire knowledge, skills, and values for the benefit of mankind.
